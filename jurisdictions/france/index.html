Executive Summary

French bookkeeping is governed by the Commercial Code, French GAAP and the Plan Comptable Général (PCG), which define how transactions must be recorded, classified and presented.[web:164][web:175][web:177][web:178]

Accounts must be kept in French and in euros, using the PCG chart of accounts and mandatory accounting books (journal and general ledger) to record transactions chronologically and by account.[web:164][web:168][web:175][web:177]

Each accounting entry must be supported by relevant documentation and integrated into an audit trail; during a tax audit, businesses must provide a compliant FEC file and may face minimum fines and tax penalties if the file is missing or non-compliant.[web:169][web:175][web:177]

From 2026, France is implementing phased mandatory B2B e‑invoicing and e‑reporting, meaning bookkeeping will increasingly be driven by structured electronic invoices, digital reporting flows and probative archiving.[web:169][web:170][web:175]

Purpose

The purpose of French bookkeeping is to produce reliable, PCG-compliant financial information, support tax and legal reporting and ensure that accounts can be defended in audits and legal proceedings.[web:164][web:167][web:175][web:177]

It serves as the foundation for annual financial statements, management reporting and digital tax controls via FEC and e‑reporting.[web:169][web:175][web:177]

Primary Outcome

Accurate and timely French bookkeeping, including PCG-aligned entries in French, up-to-date journal and general ledger, compliant FEC, and annual accounts that can be filed and, where required, published.[web:164][web:168][web:169][web:175][web:177]

Regulatory & Operational Framework

The framework section identifies rule layers that define French bookkeeping practice.[web:164][web:167][web:175][web:177][web:178]

Commercial Code and Accounting Rules Set general accounting obligations, including keeping books in French, maintaining journal and general ledger and producing annual accounts.[web:168][web:174][web:178]
Plan Comptable Général (PCG) Provides the French chart of accounts and principles updated by ANC; French GAAP must be applied to local operations.[web:164][web:175][web:177]
French GAAP vs IFRS French GAAP (PCG) applies to statutory accounts; IFRS may apply for consolidated group reporting but cannot replace PCG for French statutory accounts.[web:175][web:177]
FEC Regulations Require production of an electronic accounting file with precise structure; non-compliance triggers fines and tax penalties.[web:169][web:175][web:177]
E-invoicing and E-reporting Regime Introduces mandatory B2B e‑invoicing and transaction e‑reporting, affecting how bookkeeping data is captured and reported.[web:169][web:170][web:175]
Process Flow

Process flow explains how bookkeeping progresses from business events to PCG‑compliant accounts and digital files.[web:164][web:167][web:168][web:169][web:171][web:175][web:177]

1. Configure Chart of Accounts Set up PCG-based accounts, and if part of a group, configure dual charts mapping foreign accounts to French account numbers.[web:164][web:175][web:177]
2. Record Transactions in Journal Record all business transactions chronologically in the journal, with each entry in French and in euros, supported by a voucher.[web:164][web:168][web:171][web:177]
3. Post to General Ledger Organise entries by account in the general ledger, ensuring reconciliation with journal and subledgers.[web:164][web:168][web:177]
4. Trial Balance and Adjustments Produce trial balance, perform PCG adjustments and separate French entries from foreign group entries where necessary.[web:164][web:175][web:177]
5. Annual Accounts Prepare balance sheet, income statement and notes at year‑end, and publish accounts with the commercial court registry where required.[web:164][web:167][web:175]
6. FEC Generation Generate compliant FEC for the fiscal year from the accounting system, ensuring structure and mapping meet tax requirements.[web:169][web:175][web:177]
7. E-invoicing & E-reporting Send structured electronic invoices and digital transaction reports via authorised platforms as obligations roll out.[web:169][web:170][web:175]
