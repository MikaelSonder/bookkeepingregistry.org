Executive Summary

All enterprises in Belgium must keep accounts, with two broad categories: SMEs, which must keep full double-entry accounts and may submit abbreviated annual accounts, and large businesses, which must submit full annual accounts.[web:149][web:153][web:156][web:158]

Proper bookkeeping is a legal requirement and involves recording all income and expenses with supporting documents, either in single/simplified bookkeeping for certain small entities or double-entry bookkeeping for companies and larger businesses.[web:150][web:159]

Accounting documents and all documents determining taxable income must be retained for seven years, while from 2026 Belgian VAT-liable entities must issue structured electronic B2B invoices via Peppol, making e-invoicing part of practical bookkeeping.[web:149][web:152]

Belgian companies must present annual accounts drafted in accordance with Belgian GAAP, with IFRS mandatory for consolidated accounts of listed companies and optional for some other consolidated accounts, and foreign branches must keep separate Belgian bookkeeping and file copies of financial statements.[web:156][web:158][web:160][web:161]

Purpose

The purpose of the bookkeeping function in Belgium is to demonstrate that the business operates smoothly, provide a reliable basis for tax and social contributions and support preparation and publication of annual accounts.[web:150][web:156][web:159]

It converts legal obligations into organised accounts and documentation that can be inspected by authorities, auditors and stakeholders.[web:149][web:156][web:158]

Primary Outcome

Accurate and timely Belgian bookkeeping, including complete ledgers, supporting documents retained for seven years, compliant annual accounts and alignment with e-invoicing obligations.[web:149][web:150][web:152][web:156][web:158]

Country Characteristics

Country characteristics explain features that shape bookkeeping in Belgium: legal form and turnover drive method choice, and annual accounts and publication duties are tightly regulated.[web:149][web:150][web:156][web:158][web:159][web:160][web:161]

Process Flow

The process flow explains how bookkeeping normally progresses from business event to records and reporting in Belgium.[web:150][web:156][web:158][web:159][web:160]

1. Determine Method Decide whether simplified or double-entry bookkeeping applies based on legal form and annual turnover.[web:150][web:159]
2. Record Income and Expenses Record all income and expenses with corresponding supporting documents.[web:150]
3. Maintain Ledgers For simplified: purchase, sales, financial and inventory ledgers; for double-entry: journal, chart of accounts and annual inventory.[web:150]
4. VAT and Tax Returns Use accounts to prepare periodic VAT returns and annual tax declarations.[web:159]
5. Annual Accounts Preparation Prepare annual accounts according to Belgian GAAP, approve them and file online with NBB when required.[web:156][web:158][web:159][web:161]
6. E-invoicing Integration Implement Peppol-compatible invoicing software and send structured B2B invoices electronically.[web:152]
7. Archiving Retain accounting documents and tax-relevant documents for seven years.[web:149][web:153][web:162]
